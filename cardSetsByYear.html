Ah...the '90s
Miami Vice, acid-wash jeans, those teal San Jose Sharks jerseys...and a hockey card hobby boom. Consider the 1989-90 NHL season featured exactly two main sets - Topps for the US market, and O-Pee-Chee for Canada. Three years later there were thirteen (13!) sets between the US and Canadian sets - sixteen if you include the French versions of the Upper Deck & Parkhurst sets plus McDonald's. You can see why, with the sheer volume of cards produced during these heady days, the era the "Junk Wax" moniker.

But there are some hidden gems to be found if you are willing to sift through the rubble. So let's take a trip down memory lane shall we?
