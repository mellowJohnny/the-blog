The Junk Wax Review...

If you are asking what "Junk Wax" let me take a moment to wax nostalgic for a moment...

The Junk Wax era was a dark period in the history or sport card collecting, spanning roughly from the mid-80s to about 1993-94. At the time everyone seemed to be collecting. In Montreal where I grew up, card shops were eveywhere, there were multiple, competing cards shows every week-end (even mid-week shows became a thing), Beckett was the hockey card pricing bible (this was pre-internet of course) and collectors looked forward to each new monthly issue to see if the cards in their set had gone up or down. Counterfit cards - specifically Gretzky, Lemieux, Roy and Brett Hull rookies - were creeping into the hobby.

And althought it was short-lived, it almost killed the hobby. Some cards companies didn't survive. It also coinsides with the time I began collecting in earnest, starting with the 1991-92 Upper Deck Hockey series...

What you will find below is a review of (almost) every hockey set from that era (even McDonalds!). And while some look back on the Junk Wax era with distain, it hol;d fond memories for me, and more importantly, not all sets were "junk", many have stood the test of time to become little gems. The sets & rookies form the era may not be helping put your kids through college, but there are still some nice, affordable sets left behind. And personally I find it disappointing that as a result of the bankruptcies & consolidations the hobby has basically collapsed into a single mega-card company - Upper Deck. So for me the Junk Wax era has one thing going for it - the sheer amount of choice & variety of sets at the time. So dig in!
